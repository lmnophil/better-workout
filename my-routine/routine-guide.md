# Your Training Guide

A read-while-you-train companion. It explains *what* you're doing, *why*, and *how to do it
well* — so the routine becomes something you understand, not just a list you follow. Skim the
section you need; the one-page cheat sheet is at the bottom.

Built from your two fitness 1:1s (the GWE notes) + your own goals, equipment, and lifestyle.

---

## 1. What this plan is for

Five objectives, in priority order:

1. **Undo the desk.** You sit 8–10+ hours a day. That's a health risk *on its own*, separate
   from whether you train — so the highest-value thing in this whole plan isn't any single lift,
   it's **breaking up sitting all day long** (the walking desk + movement snacks). Don't skip it
   because it feels too easy. It isn't.
2. **Be around, and able, for a long time.** Strength, mobility, balance, and posture are the
   pillars of aging well. This plan trains all four, not just the fun one.
3. **Keep up with the kid.** Real-world capacity: pick things up off the floor, carry a car seat,
   get up and down, have an engine. That's hinges, carries, squats, and easy cardio.
4. **Lift the barbell, and get better at it.** You enjoy the squat, deadlift, bench, overhead
   press, and row — so they're the *spine* of every week, and you'll actually progress on them.
5. **Stay balanced.** The app tracks weekly volume per muscle and flags what you've neglected.
   The plan is built to keep every muscle in its target band, not just the show muscles.

**The key insight that makes it cohere:** your instructors' plan (mobility, glute/scap activation,
landmine work, balance, foam rolling) and your barbell lifts are *two halves of one whole*. The
barbell lifts cover the big movers — quads, glutes, back, chest, shoulders — efficiently. The
instructor work covers exactly what desk-bound lifters neglect — posture, the small scapular and
rear-delt muscles, hips, balance, tissue quality. Do only one half and you have a gap. Together
they're complete. That's why this is a *hybrid*, not a compromise.

## 2. The principles (learn these and you can coach yourself)

**Consolidate intensity, distribute the rest.** Heavy barbell work needs a warm-up and a focused
block — keep it together. Mobility, foam rolling, posture/glute activation, and sitting-breaks are
*better* in small frequent doses, so spread them through the day. This isn't a hack to fit your
schedule; it's how you'd train even with unlimited time.

**Volume is the driver.** Strength and muscle come mostly from *total weekly working sets per
muscle*, accumulated over time — not from any one brutal session. Hitting each muscle about
**twice a week** (which the split does) is the frequency that matters.

**Progressive overload is the engine.** Muscles adapt to a stress slightly beyond what they've
seen. So over weeks you add a little — more weight, or more reps at the same weight. No overload,
no change. Section 8 is the how.

**Technique before load, always.** A clean rep at a lighter weight beats a grindy ugly one. Add
weight only when the movement is crisp. Tap the **play icon** on any exercise in the app for a
demo video if you're unsure of a pattern.

**Leave reps in the tank (RIR).** "Reps in reserve" = how many more you *could* have done. Most
of your sets should stop with **1–3 RIR** — not to failure. This is also your auto-pilot for bad
days (next principle).

**Recovery is where you adapt — and a newborn will wreck it.** You get stronger between sessions,
not during. With broken sleep coming, you can't train like a fresh 22-year-old. The RIR target
handles this automatically: on a trashed-sleep day, "stop at 2 RIR" naturally means less weight or
fewer reps. Don't chase a number when you're running on empty. A missed day costs nothing; protect
the *next* good one.

**Warm up the movement, not just the body.** Because your mobility/activation is done through the
day, your gym warm-up is short: a couple of light ramp-up sets of the first lift. That's enough
when you walked in already primed.

## 3. The shape of a day (bookended)

You train in **bookends**: small stuff spread through the day, one real block after work.

| | When | ~Time | What |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Workday layer** | through the day | ~15 min | Sitting-breaks, a morning joint primer, glute + posture micro-sets. *Highest ROI.* |
| **The anchor** | after work | 30–40 min | The day's strength block (the barbell + accessories). |
| **Evening layer** | before bed | ~10–15 min | Foam rolling, one long stretch, a balance hold. *Optional.* |

### Train to the day you actually got — three tiers
- **🟢 Green (it all clicks, ~60 min):** all three layers. The full day.
- **🟡 Yellow (busy/tired, ~30 min):** skip the evening; cut the lift to the **top 3 exercises**
  of that day (marked below); keep a couple of sitting-breaks. You still bank the main stimulus.
- **🔴 Red (no sleep / no time / chaos, ~10 min):** the workday layer *only* — a few
  sitting-breaks, 5 min of mobility, a walk. That's not failure, that's maintenance. You lose
  nothing in a day.

The point of bookending: the anchor is small enough to defend, and everything else flexes.

## 4. The shape of a week

Four lifting days, run as a **self-paced cycle** (the app's "sequence" mode), in this order:

**Lower A → Upper A → Lower B → Upper B → (repeat)**

"Today's workout" is just the next one in the cycle, *whenever* you show up. Miss days, miss a
week — you never fall behind a calendar. Aim for **4 lifts a week**; the other ~3 days are the
workday + evening layers plus an easy cardio session. The order already alternates lower/upper, so
even two lifting days back-to-back are fine.

## 5. The four training days

**Do every exercise in a day — a day is one full session, not a menu.** The only thing you *pick
between* is a *(rotating)* slot, which is a **pool**: there you do one of a few interchangeable
options and the app suggests which (see "Rotating accessories" below). The **bold top-3 "keepers"**
are *only* your Yellow-day fallback when you're out of time — not the normal plan.

Order is top-to-bottom, and the app groups each day by module: light activation/prep first (so some
core and posture work sits *before* the barbell as bracing/joint prep — intentional and kept light),
then the barbell mains, then accessories, then a balance/core finish. During a session you can
perform in any order you like. Sets × reps are starting points — let Section 8 move them.

| **Romanian deadlift** | 3–4 × 8 |
| **Bulgarian squat (seated step away)** *(rotating)* | 3 × 8 / side |
| Hollow hold | 3 × 20–30s |
| Single-leg balance hold *(finisher)* | 2 × 30s / side |

### Upper A — Bench & Row day
*Objective: horizontal push + pull, plus rear-delt/posture.*

| Banded face pulls | 3 × 15 |
| Bicep curl *(rotating)* | 3 × 10 |
| Bench dips *(rotating, finisher)* | 3 × AMRAP |

### Lower B — Deadlift day

*Objective: overhead push + pull, shoulder health, posture.*

| Exercise | Sets × reps |
|---|---|

| **Dumbbell bench press** *(flat, rotating)* | 3 × 10 |

| Prone Y raises | 3 × 10 |
| Hammer curl + Side plank *(finisher)* | 3 × 10 / 3 × 30s |

### Form cues for the anchors
Short reminders — use the app's demo video for the full picture.

- **Back squat:** brace your whole trunk before you unrack; sit between your hips to depth; knees
  track over toes; drive the floor away. Bar over mid-foot the whole time.
- **Romanian deadlift:** soft knees, push your hips *back* (not down), bar drags down your thighs,
  feel the hamstrings load; stop when your back wants to round, stand tall.
- **Conventional deadlift:** bar over mid-foot, shins to bar, take the slack out ("pull the bar
  into you") before you lift; hips and shoulders rise together; finish standing tall, don't lean
  back.
- **Front squat:** elbows *up* and proud, bar rests on the shelf of your front delts (fingers just
  guide it), stay upright, full depth.
- **Bench press:** shoulder blades pinned down and back, slight arch, bar to lower chest, elbows
  ~45° (not flared), drive your feet into the floor.
- **Overhead press:** ribs down and glutes tight (don't lean back), bar over mid-foot, press and
  move your head "through the window" at lockout.
- **Barbell row:** hinge to ~45°, flat back, pull to your lower ribs and lead with the elbow,
  control it down. **Landmine row** is the same intent with the 47" bar — drive the elbow back,
  neutral spine.

### Rotating accessories (pools)
Your **main lifts never rotate** — squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press, row, front squat, RDL,
pull-ups, and landmine row stay fixed every week, because getting stronger on them depends on
repeating them and beating last time.

The slots marked *(rotating)* above are **pools**: the app offers 2–3 interchangeable options and you
do **one**, and it serves whichever you've done least recently. That buys variety, spreads load
across your joints over the years, and lets you grab the easier option on a rough day — without
unbalancing anything, since every pool's options train the same muscle. The exact members and setup
are in `app-setup.md`.

## 6. The distributed layer (the instructors' work)

This is where the GWE plan lives — pushed off the clock so the anchor stays short. The app tracks
these by **recency** (the Coverage view), so the goal is "touched recently," not a set count.

**Morning joint primer (~4 min)** — bias to today's lift:
- Before a **lower** day: 90/90 hip switches → Deep squat with reach → Ankle rocks → World's greatest stretch.
- Before an **upper** day: Cat-cow → Thread the needle → Banded shoulder dislocates → Doorway pec stretch.

**Desk-break resets (2–3 × ~3 min through the workday)** — keep a band at the desk; always land
*one posture + one glute* touch:
- **Posture:** Scapular postural band work + Banded pull-aparts + Banded face pulls.
- **Glutes/hips:** Banded glute bridges with abduction + Lateral band walks + Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch.
- **Just get vertical:** a few minutes on the treadmill at ~2% incline, or a lap + bodyweight squats.

**Evening wind-down (~10–15 min, optional):** foam roll 1–2 areas (rotate quads, IT band, calves,
lats, T-spine, paraspinals) + one long stretch (Couch stretch or banded Hamstring stretch, slow
nasal breathing) + a Single-leg balance hold while brushing your teeth.

> Your instructors' "daily non-negotiables" — one posture reset and one glute activation every day
> — are satisfied entirely by the desk-break resets. The scapular band work alone carries most of
> your weekly posture volume, which is why the lifting days don't need to cram it.

## 7. Cardio (counted separately)

- **Walking desk** at ~2% incline — your default work setup. This is the desk-undoing engine; that
  small incline recruits the posterior chain and meaningfully changes the demand vs. flat.
- **Concept2, Zone 2, 1–2× / week** — conversational pace, 20–40 min. Great longevity/aerobic base
  at low joint cost. Do it on a non-lifting day, or at least a few hours from your lift (hard
  cardio too close to lifting blunts strength gains — the "interference effect").
- **Optional Rev-Up finisher** (Farmer carry, Jump lunges, Leaning wall jog) only on a Green day
  with time to spare.

## 8. How to get stronger without burning out

Two simple rules, plus an auto-pilot.

**Accessories & higher-rep work (8–15 reps) → double progression.** Pick a rep range (e.g.,
3 × 8–12). Keep the weight until you hit the *top* of the range on *all* sets at ~1–2 RIR. Then add
the smallest increment and drop back to the bottom of the range. Repeat. (Example: 3×8 → 3×9 → … →
3×12 → add weight → 3×8 again.)

**Main barbell lifts (3–5 reps) → small jumps when it's crisp.**
- Start lighter than your ego wants; nail technique first.
- If every working set felt solid at ~1–2 RIR, add a little next time: **~5 lb** upper-body
  (bench, OHP), **~5–10 lb** lower-body (squat, deadlift).
- If you ground out reps or form slipped, **repeat the same weight** next time.
- Stall twice at a weight? **Back off ~10%** and build up again — you'll blow past it.

**RIR is the auto-pilot.** Holding "1–3 reps in reserve" means the weight self-adjusts to how
recovered you are. Bad-sleep week → the bar feels heavy → you do less at the same RIR, on purpose.
This is the single most important habit for training through new-parenthood.

**Deloads.** Every ~6–8 weeks (or when joints ache / sleep is shot), take an easy week — roughly
half the sets, lighter weight, clean reps. Honestly, with a newborn, *life will deload you*; the
skill is letting it instead of fighting it.

**Expect uneven progress.** Lower-body climbs faster than upper; press strength crawls. Some weeks
you just maintain. Maintaining *is* winning when you're sleep-deprived.

## 9. Reading your progress in the app

- **"Last time" on each exercise** = your target to beat or match. The simplest progression habit:
  glance at last time, try to add a rep or a little weight within the rules above.
- **Coverage view** = your balance check. Volume-tracked muscles show weekly sets vs. target;
  mobility/balance/soft-tissue show recency (did you touch them lately?). If something's red, that's
  your nudge — usually it means the distributed layer slipped, not the lifting.
- **Volume vs. target bars** confirm the week is balanced. You should see the big movers near
  target from the lifts, and posture/mobility kept green by the desk-breaks.

## 10. When to back off

- **Sharp or joint pain ≠ muscle soreness.** Soreness in the belly of a muscle is normal; sharp,
  pinching, or joint-line pain means stop that movement and reassess. Don't train through it.
- **Form breaking down** is your set's real endpoint — rep quality over rep count.
- **Sleep/stress in the gutter** → drop to a Yellow or Red day without guilt. The plan is built to
  absorb that.
- **Hydration** — your nutrition 1:1 flagged it: roughly half your bodyweight in ounces of water a
  day, more around training. Easy lever, real recovery payoff.

## One-page cheat sheet

**Week (self-paced cycle):** Lower A → Upper A → Lower B → Upper B → repeat. Aim 4 lifts/week.

**Each day:** workday movement snacks → 30–40 min anchor after work → optional evening wind-down.

**Tiers:** 🟢 all three layers · 🟡 top-3 lifts + a couple snacks · 🔴 snacks/walk only.

**The anchors (top-3 keepers in bold):**
- **Lower A:** **Back squat · Romanian deadlift · Bulgarian squat** · Hollow hold · balance
- **Upper A:** **Bench press · Barbell row · Pull-ups** · Face pulls · Curl · Bench dips
- **Lower B:** **Deadlift · Front squat · DB walking lunges** · DB hip thrust · Pallof press
- **Upper B:** **Overhead press · Landmine row · DB bench** · Lateral raises · Y raises · Hammer curl + Side plank

**Do the whole day** — bold = time-crunch keepers only. *(rotating)* slots are pools (pick 1; the app suggests which); members in `app-setup.md`.

**Progress:** mains → +5 lb (upper) / +5–10 lb (lower) when crisp at 1–2 RIR, else repeat.
Accessories → double progression (top of range on all sets, then add weight). Always leave 1–3 RIR.

**Don't skip:** breaking up sitting all day. It's the highest-ROI thing here.
